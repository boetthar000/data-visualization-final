These charts show the mobile cellular subscriptions in certain countries over the course of 6 years.

The purpose of these charts is suppose to give an idea of different mobile cellular subscriptions per continent from 2008 to 2013.

The Left chart shows the difference in "North America", as you can see United States is the the top cellular subscription.
The middle chart shows the data in Europe, from the diagram we can see it is followed with Italy then with Germany.
The Right chart shows the visulization of data in Asia, from east Asia to west Asia. We can see the leading country to be China which also contains one of the most amount of people in the world.

The purpose of these donut charts is suppose to represent different mobile cellular subscriptions, throughout the largest populated countries from 2008 to 2013.

The Left chart represents United States which has 339,665,118 people.
The middle chart shows the data in China with 1,413,142,846 people.
The Right chart shows the visulization of data in India with 1,399,179,585 people.
China is the number one population as of now followed by India, and then United States. We can see that India had a smaller cellular subscription then incresed as the time progressed.
